Getting Started

On this page

Welcome to Toku Budget — a fast, private budgeting app that keeps your data local, with optional iCloud sync for Premium. This guide walks you through your first 10 minutes.

Quick start (5 steps)

  1. Pick a date range. Use Month / Quarter / Year in the top bar.
  2. Add transactions. Go to Transactions → add manually or Import CSV….
  3. Create budgets. Open Budgets and set monthly limits per category.
  4. Log subscriptions & bills. Use Subscriptions and Bills; mark paid to roll forward.
  5. Review the Overview. The Net Balance card, Daily Spend, and Breakdown summarize your month.

First run & navigation

The sidebar holds the main sections: Overview, Transactions, Subscriptions, Bills, Budgets, Stats, Trends, Coach. A settings gear lives at the bottom of the sidebar. You’ll also see a top toolbar with your date range, currency chips (USD now; JPY shows a lock and “coming soon”), and a Savings Goal button.

Add your first data

Manual entry

Go to Transactions and add an income or expense. Choose a category, amount, date, note, and currency (USD).

Import from CSV

On macOS, open the toolbar menu Import/Export → Import CSV… (or use Settings → Import). Map your columns in the preview and pick an Amount Mode:

  • Debit/Credit Columns — separate columns for money out / in.
  • Single Amount — one column (negatives are expenses; positives are income).

Choose the correct date format, then import. Toku Budget automatically skips duplicates using a content hash (date + amount + kind + currency + note).

Tip: You can save your mapping as a reusable template; it’ll auto-apply next time when headers match.

Categories

Starter categories are created for you. Edit them in Settings → Categories. Categories drive your Budgets and reports, so keep them simple and meaningful.

Budgets

In Budgets, add a monthly limit per category. You’ll see ring charts showing % used. The Overview card also surfaces two categories for a quick glance.

Subscriptions & Bills

Track recurring charges in Subscriptions and due dates in Bills. When you hit Mark Paid, the next cycle is scheduled (monthly or yearly). If reminders are enabled, Toku Budget schedules local notifications ahead of the due date.

Savings Goal

Click Savings Goal in the toolbar to set a target amount and a target date. Suggestions and trend tips will use this to guide monthly targets.

Understand the Overview

  • Net Balance — income minus expenses for the current range, with a simple running trend chart and zero line.
  • Upcoming Bills — your next payments at a glance; mark paid right there.
  • By Category — a quick breakdown of where money is going.
  • Recent — latest transactions (double-click to edit on macOS).
  • Daily Spending — see spikes by day to spot patterns.
  • AI Tips (Premium) — personalized suggestions based on your activity.

Click the ? button in the toolbar to replay the coach marks that highlight key UI areas.

Appearance & currency

Pick Light or Dark in Settings → Appearance and choose an accent color (more colors unlock with Premium). The currency chip currently supports USD. JPY is shown but locked with a “coming soon” message.

iCloud Sync (Premium)

Enable in Settings → Sync. You can see the last sync time and trigger a manual sync. Data is stored locally with Core Data; Premium adds cloud sync between devices signed into the same Apple ID.

Import & export

Import is available to everyone. CSV export is available from the macOS menu and Settings → Export and requires Premium.

Handy shortcuts (macOS)

  • ⌘ , — Open Settings
  • Toolbar → Import/Export — Import CSV, Export CSV (Premium)

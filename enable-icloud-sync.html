Enable iCloud Sync

On this page

Toku Budget keeps everything local by default (fast, private). With Premium you can turn on iCloud Sync to keep your data consistent across devices signed in to the same Apple ID.

What iCloud Sync does

  • Syncs your budgeting data between your Mac and other Apple devices using your iCloud account.
  • Keeps the same categories, transactions, budgets, subscriptions and bills available on each device.
  • Still works offline; changes sync when you’re back online.
Your data stays in your private iCloud — Toku Budget doesn’t have access to your account.

Turn on iCloud Sync

  1. Open Settings in Toku Budget.
  2. Select Sync.
  3. Toggle Sync with iCloud on.
  4. (Optional) Click Sync Now to run a manual sync. The Last Sync time shows the most recent run.

If you don’t have Premium, the Sync page will indicate that it’s a Premium feature and offer an upgrade.

Requirements

  • Active Toku Budget Premium on the device.
  • Signed in to the same Apple ID on each device.
  • iCloud Drive enabled in System Settings.
  • Network connection for changes to upload/download (offline edits queue up).

Tips & good to know

  • You can leave sync off and use Toku Budget entirely offline — your data remains on-device.
  • Deleting data on one device and syncing will remove it from your other devices too.
  • If a device looks out of date, open Toku Budget and use Sync Now on that device.

Troubleshooting

  • Don’t see the Sync toggle? Make sure you’re on the latest version and that Premium is active.
  • Sync seems stuck? Toggle Sync with iCloud off, then back on, and tap Sync Now.
  • Different Apple IDs? Devices must be signed into the same Apple ID to share the same data.
  • iCloud Drive off? Enable iCloud Drive in macOS/iOS settings and try again.

Privacy & control

Sync is optional and can be disabled at any time in Settings → Sync. Your data is yours: it’s stored locally with Core Data and, if enabled, in your personal iCloud. We do not see or sell your data.
